FCEVs means Fuel Cell Electric Vehicles.
It is an electric vehicle that uses fuel cell to power the motor.

Introduction

Energy is a factor which determine a country’s economy, transportation, and standard of life. The problem faced globally is the consumption and the availability of energy.

There are two main problems of continuous using of fossil fuels -
1) Limited amount of fossil fuel.
2) Cause due to fossil fuel to environment such as Global Warming, Acid Rain, Pollution, etc.

To supply more amount of energy for increasing population, alternative is to u-se electricity for vehicles.

It is the cleanest and efficient fuel.

The alternative to fossil fuels represented by other energy sources such as: battery systems, ultracapacitors or fuel cells.

FCEVs can reduce emission to some extent but not to zero.

Thus, the renewable energy transport allows FCEVs to become preferable choice.

Information

Fuel cells in vehicles generate electricity generally using oxygen from the air and compressed hydrogen.

Most of the fuel cell vehicles are classifies as zero emissions vehicle that emits only heat and water.

There are various types of fuel cell systems. But the principle function is same.

Principle of FCEVs

For fuel cell system anode, cathode and electrolyte are required.
The electrolyte is in between the cathode and the anode.
Fuel (hydrogen) is in the anode compartment of the fuel cell and the air or pure oxygen is in the cathode compartment.
On anode side of cell, electrons are separated as the gas tries to make its way through the electrolyte membrane.
Membrane act as filter to separate hydrogen ions and electrons.
In this way, electricity is formed and supplied to motor to drive the wheels.
Water as well as heat is the by-product of the fuel cell.
